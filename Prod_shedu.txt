PROBLEM:
A bakery needs to schedule 30 customer orders over 10 days across 4 production lines and 2 packaging lines. Each order must go through production first, then packaging, in sequence. Not all machines are available from day one. Inventory partially covers demand, so only the shortfall needs to be produced. Every order has a release time and a deadline. No two orders can run on the same machine at the same time. Minimize total tardiness across all orders.

SCHEMA:
Four tables are provided as CSVs.
products — one row per product with its ID, name, minutes to produce one unit, and current inventory stock.
orders — one row per customer order with its ID, the product ordered, customer name, and quantity requested. Production time is max(0, demand_qty - inventory_qty) * processing_time_min_per_unit. Packaging time is 20% of that, rounded up.
timelines — one row per order with the earliest day and minute it can start, and the day and minute by which packaging must be complete. Convert to absolute minutes using day * 480 + minute_offset.
machines — one row per machine with its ID, name, stage (production or packaging), daily capacity in minutes, and the first and last day it is available.
